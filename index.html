About Me

I completed my PhD in Computer Science at the University of Kentucky. My research lies at the forefront of deep learning, multimodal learning, and real-world data challenges to build highly effective AI solutions. During my PhD, I interned at Google (≈9 months), leading the development of multi-agent systems and foundational architectures like TFRBench (ICML 2026) and STRIDE. I was also selected for the exclusive Meta PhD Forum.

My research has achieved broad impact across both industry and academia, leading to publications in venues such as ICML, AAAI, KDD, ECAI, Nature Communications, Information Fusion, Journal of Biomedical Informatics, and Medical Image Analysis.

Previously, I earned my BSc from RUET and lectured at Green University of Bangladesh. As a Kaggle Competition Expert, I also enjoy solving practical ML problems. I’m always interested in new ideas and exciting collaborations!
